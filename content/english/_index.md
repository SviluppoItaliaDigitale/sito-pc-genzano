## Welcome

The **Civil Protection Volunteers Group of Genzano di Roma** is a public service of the Municipality. We help citizens before, during and after emergencies — earthquakes, floods, wildfires, severe weather. Our work is **volunteer and free**.

This page gives you **essential information** in English. The full website is in Italian (use your browser's translation feature for the rest).

<div class="en-card">
<h2>📞 Emergency number</h2>
<p>For <strong>any emergency</strong> in Italy, call:</p>
<a href="tel:112" class="en-tel">📞 Call 112</a>
<p><strong>112</strong> is the European Single Emergency Number. It is:</p>
<ul class="en-list">
<li><strong>Free</strong>, even without credit on your SIM</li>
<li>Available <strong>across the European Union</strong></li>
<li>Connected to fire brigade, ambulance, police and Carabinieri</li>
<li>Working <strong>without app or internet</strong></li>
</ul>
<p>When the operator answers, say:</p>
<ol class="en-list">
<li><strong>What happened</strong> (accident / fire / medical / crime)</li>
<li><strong>Where you are</strong> (street, number, town)</li>
<li><strong>If anyone is hurt</strong></li>
</ol>
<p>Stay calm, speak slowly, do <strong>not hang up</strong> until the operator does.</p>
</div>

<div class="en-card">
<h2>🌍 Earthquake</h2>
<p>Italy is a seismic country. Genzano is in <strong>seismic zone 2B</strong> (medium hazard). If you feel shaking:</p>
<ul class="en-list">
<li>Take cover under a sturdy table or against an interior wall</li>
<li>Cover your head with your hands</li>
<li>Stay where you are until the shaking stops</li>
<li>Do <strong>not</strong> run outside immediately</li>
<li>Do <strong>not</strong> use elevators</li>
<li>Stay away from windows and bookcases</li>
</ul>
<p>After the shaking, exit calmly through stairs and reach the closest <strong>"Area di Attesa"</strong> (waiting area). See the <a href="/cartografia/">map of waiting areas</a>.</p>
</div>

<div class="en-card">
<h2>🌧️ Heavy rain & flooding</h2>
<p>The Castelli Romani area is exposed to intense rainfall (especially May-October). If a strong storm or flood is expected:</p>
<ul class="en-list">
<li>Stay indoors at upper floors</li>
<li>Avoid basements and underground parking</li>
<li>Do <strong>not</strong> drive through flooded underpasses</li>
<li>Do <strong>not</strong> approach rivers or torrents</li>
<li>Move valuables and electrical equipment to higher floors</li>
</ul>
<p>Check the official weather alert at <a href="https://protezionecivile.regione.lazio.it/gestione-emergenze/centro-funzionale/bollettini-allertamenti" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Lazio Region</a>.</p>
</div>

<div class="en-card">
<h2>🔥 Fire</h2>
<p>If you see smoke or flames:</p>
<ul class="en-list">
<li>Call <strong>112</strong> immediately</li>
<li>Leave the building through stairs (never use elevators)</li>
<li>Close doors behind you (slows the fire spread)</li>
<li>Stay low if there is smoke (cleaner air near the floor)</li>
<li>Do <strong>not</strong> go back for belongings</li>
</ul>
<p>For wildfires (June-September is high risk in Lazio), report immediately by calling 112.</p>
</div>

<div class="en-card">
<h2>⛈️ Thunderstorms</h2>
<p>Sudden intense storms with lightning are common in summer. During a storm:</p>
<ul class="en-list">
<li>Stay indoors</li>
<li>Close windows and unplug appliances</li>
<li>Do <strong>not</strong> shelter under trees</li>
<li>Do <strong>not</strong> swim in pools or lakes</li>
<li>Avoid rivers and underpasses</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="en-card">
<h2>📦 Emergency kit at home</h2>
<p>Keep a small backpack ready with:</p>
<ul class="en-list">
<li>Bottled water (3 liters per person)</li>
<li>Long-life food (biscuits, energy bars)</li>
<li>Flashlight with spare batteries</li>
<li>Thermal blanket and whistle</li>
<li>Phone charger</li>
<li>Important medications</li>
<li>Copy of ID documents</li>
<li>Cash (small amount)</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="en-card">
<h2>📱 IT-alert</h2>
<p>Italy has a national alert system called <strong>IT-alert</strong>. In case of major emergency it sends a loud message directly to all phones in the affected area, even without app, internet, or signal from your provider. The message is in Italian and English. More info: <a href="https://www.it-alert.it/en/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">it-alert.it</a>.</p>
</div>

<div class="en-card">
<h2>🗺️ Where to go in emergency</h2>
<p>Genzano has <strong>10 designated waiting areas</strong> (Aree di Attesa) where citizens should gather after a major event. They are open public spaces, marked with signs. Find the closest to your home or hotel before an emergency happens.</p>
